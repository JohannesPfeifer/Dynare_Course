\begin{frame}{Model applications: Principles}
\begin{itemize}
    \item Main use of models:
    \begin{itemize}
        \item Explaining developments ex post (estimated models)
        \item Quantitative projections: counterfactuals (``what if'') and forecast scenarios
    \end{itemize}
\item All models are simplifications of reality (hopefully sufficiently accurate and useful ones in the context of the analytical question)
\item In ECFIN policy work we spend much time on translating policy scenarios into scenarios that can be simulated with the model
\begin{itemize}
        \item Structural reform measures do often not directly correspond to model variables (contrary to standard fiscal policy measures) 
        \item Need to proxy the measures in this case by their economic substance (using ``satellite'' tools)    
    \end{itemize}
\end{itemize}
\end{frame}
